Post Mortem

What went well: Overall, we were quite proud of our Pac-Man game. We were able to incorporate many of the different object-oriented design patterns taught within the course, while also taking advantage of a component-based and data-driven architecture. Our codebase was well fleshed-out and is well-equipped for future iterations.

What could be improved: Given 8 more weeks, we would have fleshed out the Pac-Man's pathfinding abilities to exactly mirror those of the original Pac-Man game. We would have loved to be able to incorporate some more uniqueness to our project. Some ideas we had included a boss mode, where there would be a special ghost that had more speed than the others. Others included adding in more unique powerups such as a speed-boost or ghost slow-down. From a UI perspective, we also would have loved to make our game more visually pleasing and have added an introductory title scene and an end scene right before the program shuts down.

Outside of gameplay specific changes, we would have loved to have more time to flesh our the internal game engine itself. Currently, our tilemap editor only allows for the editing of the scene itself. A future improvement would have been adding in functionality for our editor to take in input files, allowing users to change sprites, animation patterns, font styles, and even different audio sound bites.
